About Ozuchi village

Location

 Ozuchi village is located in Kaga-city, the southern part of Ishikawa prefecture.
(touch the image) (Hover the mouse cursor on the image.)
 Kaga-city is known for its Onsen (spa), known as Kaga-Onsen, consisting of three different Onsen: Yamanaka, Katayamazu and Yamashiro.
 Many tourists from around the world visit here to experience the Japanese Onsen, as well as to taste the delicious seafood found only from Japanese sea.

Severe depopulation

In 1883, the population of Ozuchi village had reached a peak of 297 people and 46 households. From 1894 until 1902, 24 households moved to Hokkaido Ishikari-city as a Hokkaido development group. In addition, as gas expanded as the main energy source in Japan, Ozuchi, whose main industry was charcoal-making at that time, experienced a severe depopulation from the 1950s onwards. In 2020, only 1 household remained in Ozuchi village.

National Important Preservation District

 Ozuchi village, whose population has now decreased to only 1 people, is facing a crisis of disappearance.
 On the other hand, Higashidani district, in which Ozuchi belongs to, is trying their best to maintain the unique traditional architecture and culture.
 In 2011, the Japanese goverment registered this area as a National Important Preservation District of Historic Buildings and this is attracting attention from all over Japan.

Volunteers and visitors from all over the world

Furthermore, the villager, Mr. Noboru Nimaida (so-called "Nobo-san") has been inviting lots of volunteers and visitors not only from other area of Japan but also from all over the world. As a result, total 150 people from about 10 countries visit this village per year.
